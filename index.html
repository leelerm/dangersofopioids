The Dangers of Opioids By Leah Manale
Topic

The dangers of doing opioids. How opioids can negatively effect peoples bodies and could potentially end in death from an opioid overdose.

Need for Teaching

The amount of deaths in Caucasian men has increased by 11% from the years 2012- 2017 (Healthy People 2020, 2017) This means that the need to inform people the dangers of doing opioids and how it can harm their bodies.

Information about Group/ Learner and Barriers of Learning

Most people get addicted to opioids after they have received a prescription as a pain reliever. Caucasian men have the highest overdose-related deaths most likely because they have the resources to continue their drug usage.

Learner Readiness

Interact with the patient and influence them to talk about their will to live. Once they have found that, they will be motivated to learn how to prevent an overdose and defeat their addiction.

Goal

To educate drug users of the dangers of opioid use and life saving procedures to prevent an overdose.

Objective

Patients should be able to:

  • Analyze risk factors that come with drug use.
  • Informed of the symptoms related to a drug overdose.
  • Recognize how to use proper treatments during a drug overdose.
    • Teaching Strategies

    Cognitive, Psychomotor, and Affective.

    Learning Activities

    Teach patients what naloxone is and how to use the nasal spray properly in case of an opioid overdose.

    Content

    Once the patient has found their reason to live, they will be ready to learn how to save their life during an opioid overdose.

  • The patient will learn what naloxone does to the body during an overdose.
    • It is a drug that reverses the effects of an opioid overdose by blocking the effects of opiates on the brain and restores breathing.
  • The patient will then see what a naloxone nasal spray looks like and the instructions on how to use it correctly.
    • Peel back the package and remove device.
    • Place the top of the device inside the nostril of the overdosed individual.
    • Press firmly down on the plunger to release the medication from the device into the nose.
    Evaluation

    Patient should be able to demonstrate how to properly use the naloxone nasal spray and explain, in their own words, what naloxone does to the body during the overdose.
